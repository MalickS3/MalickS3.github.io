Overview

As an everyday challenge that will keeps growing, my project will try to predict traffic patterns in New York City. This will hopefully lead to a better understanding of future traffic conditions based on historical observations..

Traffic forecasting is an important component of planning, design and operations for any city transportation network. According to the DOT, highway design and planning are usually based on estimates of traffic 20 years beyond the construction date. So while no one knows the future, trained traffic forecasters and modelers use special tools and experience to estimate these numbers

The most common existing solution is to use the traditional four steps transportation modeling which are (Trip generation, Trip distribution, Mode choice and Route assignment). Another solution is to use Travel demand modeling involves a series of mathematical models that attempt to simulate human behavior while traveling.

Data and Analysis

I created and maintain an dataset with actual driven trips for analysis purposes. The ability to match and analyze large volumes of data allowed me to make traffic predictions. Together, this creates the most accurate and up-to-date picture of the road for the most accurate and advanced traffic prediction available.

My initial discovers confirmed my beliefs that the busiest roadways are in fact highways, parkways, expressways, and other high-speed roadways. Most commonly, the three busiest roadways that stood out multiple times were the BRONX RIVER PARKWAY, LONG ISLAND EXPRESSWAY, and GRAND CENTRAL PARKWAY. The general conclusion came about analyzing the dataset revealing that the LONG ISLAND EXPRESSWAY is confirmed to be the busiest roadway within the Five Boroughs.

Multiple segments of the expressway appear within the top 10% of the city with nearly 10 millions vehicles utilizing it. This seems to make sense as the expressway (aka I-495) is an important road for trucks and cars getting from Manhattan and Queens to Long Island.

Techniques

The programs used for this project include Visual Studio and Jyputer Notebook. I first loaded the dataset and cleaned it up. Then I introduced multiple new columns for aggregating data faster. They include,

  • 4 (6 hour period) columns
  • 2 (12 hour period) columns
  • 1 (24 hour period) column
  • A morning and evening rush hour columns

After that I created several different datasets using SQL based on the year given and/or using rush hours times as well. I also created Top 10% values for multiple columns and used seaborn to visualize my data with graphs. Additionally, while there wasn't enough time,' I tried to manually add a borough column for every roadway to plot using matplotlib.
